The problem

Behavioral health clinicians are buried in admin work.

Outpatient behavioral health practices spend an enormous amount of time on documentation, billing, and scheduling. Progress notes are written after hours. Claims bounce back with preventable errors. Staff juggle multiple systems just to keep the calendar full and revenue flowing.

Manual, repetitive documentation

Clinicians re-type the same information across templates and fields, spending evenings and weekends catching up on notes instead of seeing patients or going home on time.

Fragmented billing workflows

Claims are built from data scattered across systems, increasing errors, delays, and write-offs— especially for high-volume group practices and multi-clinic organizations.

Scheduling that doesn’t talk to the EMR

Front desk teams manage calendars, reminders, and no-shows separately from clinical records, creating more manual work and missed opportunities to fill the schedule.

The solution

An AI-native EMR built to automate the entire behavioral health workflow.

Theralinx uses a two-step AI pipeline—LinxAI—to capture the key clinical, administrative, and operational details from each encounter and push them directly into the EMR. Notes draft themselves. Claims build themselves. Scheduling and documentation finally stay in sync.

1. Intelligent data extraction

LinxAI listens for the essential elements of each encounter—chief complaint, symptoms, interventions, risk, and follow-up—turning unstructured details into structured EMR fields.

2. Automated documentation

Draft progress notes are generated automatically and are ready for clinician review before the session ends, dramatically reducing after-hours work.

3. Clean claims and seamless scheduling

Documentation, billing, and scheduling operate in one synchronized system—reducing denials, preventing missed charges, and keeping calendars full with less administrative effort.
